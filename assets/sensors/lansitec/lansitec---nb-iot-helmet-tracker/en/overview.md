# LANSITEC - Nb IoT Helmet Tracker Technical Overview

## Introduction
The LANSITEC Nb IoT Helmet Tracker is a cutting-edge device designed to enhance safety and operational efficiency in various industries such as construction, mining, and logistics. With its advanced sensors and IoT capabilities, the tracker allows for real-time monitoring of helmet-wearing personnel, ensuring compliance and safety standards.

## Working Principles
The LANSITEC Nb IoT Helmet Tracker operates primarily through a combination of GPS and cellular-based Narrowband IoT (Nb-IoT) technology. This integration allows the device to:

- **Track Location:** The GPS module provides accurate positioning data, enabling real-time location tracking of personnel.
- **Monitor Helmet Usage:** Embedded sensors detect whether the helmet is being worn appropriately, using proximity and motion sensors.
- **Data Transmission:** Nb-IoT technology supports low-power, wide-area connectivity, ideal for transmitting data over long distances under challenging conditions.

## LoRaWAN Details
While primarily designed for Nb-IoT, the device can optionally support LoRaWAN for specific deployments requiring this protocol. The tracker’s LoRaWAN capabilities allow:
- **Extended Range:** Ideal for locations where Nb-IoT coverage is insufficient.
- **Lower Power Consumption:** Efficient for less frequent data transmission, conserving battery life.
- **Scalable Networks:** Suitable for large-scale deployments with numerous trackers.

## Power Consumption
The LANSITEC Nb IoT Helmet Tracker is designed for optimized energy efficiency:
- **Battery Life:** The device can last several days on a full charge, depending on usage and data transmission frequency.
- **Power-saving Modes:** It features various modes including standby and sleep to prolong battery life when not actively in use.
- **Charging:** It includes a micro-USB charging port for convenient recharging.

## Use Cases

## Limitations
- **Nb-IoT Network Dependency:** The device relies on Nb-IoT network availability which may be limited in some regions.
- **Battery Constraints:** While designed for low power consumption, frequent data transmission or continuous GPS usage may reduce battery life significantly.
- **Environmental Factors:** Extreme environmental conditions, such as high electromagnetic interference areas, may affect the accuracy of GPS and sensor data.
- **Limited Offline Functionality:** Although LoRaWAN can provide alternative connectivity, areas without adequate signal coverage may experience data transmission delays or losses.
